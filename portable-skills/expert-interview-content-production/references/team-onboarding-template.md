# Content Writer Onboarding Template

Reusable structure for onboarding a content writer into the human-in-the-loop pipeline. Customize for the specific hire, website, and content vertical.

## Doc Structure

### 1. Who You Are in This System
Explain why their human judgment matters — what AI can't do. Be specific to their strengths (writing ability, fresh eyes, taste, cultural awareness, research skills).

### 2. How Linear Works
- Task anatomy: title, description, status (Todo → In Progress → In Review → Done), priority (P0-P4)
- Where to check: Linear project URL
- Batch size: 3-5 tasks at a time

### 3. Day-to-Day Workflow
1. Check Linear first thing each work session
2. Pick up a task, move to In Progress
3. Do the work
4. Move to In Review when ready for feedback
5. When batch is mostly done, Hermes assigns the next batch
6. Communication: Linear comments for task questions, Slack/Discord for general

### 4. The Interview Pipeline (Their Role)
- Michael records expert audio answering interview questions
- Swarm transcribes
- Writer's touchpoints: review transcription for clarity, write pages from Michael's answers, final proof for voice/accuracy
- List current interview scripts ready for recording (with Linear task IDs)

### 5. Content Types & Turnaround Times
- Blog posts: 2-3 days
- Tour/activity descriptions: 1-2 days
- Social posts: same day or next day
- Email newsletters: 3-4 days

### 6. Tools
- Linear for task management
- Google Docs for drafting
- Google Drive for brand assets
- Any CMS or deployment tool
- Canva or design tool for graphics
- Team chat (Slack/Discord)

### 7. Quality Standards
- Brand voice traits with examples (specific vs generic, friendly vs corporate)
- Cultural/language notes (e.g., Hawaiian diacritical marks, local terminology)
- Fact-checking expectations
- The "insider knowledge" test: does this make the reader feel like they're getting inside info from a local friend?

### 8. First Batch
- Read existing site content to learn the voice
- Review competitor sites, note what they do well/poorly
- Read interview scripts to know what content is coming
- Write one practice piece (specify topic)

### 9. Quick Reference
- Status flow diagram
- Who to ask for what (Hermes for task questions, Michael for domain accuracy)
- The "good enough" test for moving to In Review
