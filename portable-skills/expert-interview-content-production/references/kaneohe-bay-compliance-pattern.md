# Kaneohe Bay Compliance Pattern — "Decouple and Reframe"

## The Law
HRS § 200-39 (Act 232/2023, Act 227/2024) — Kaneohe Bay Master Plan. *Prima facie* evidence: advertising commercial ocean recreation equipment for use in Kaneohe Bay is itself presumptive evidence of guilt. The state doesn't prove you operated there — the ad IS the proof, and YOU must disprove it.

## The Pattern: KBA's Proven Approach

Kailua Beach Adventures (130 Kailua Rd) has been advertising Kaneohe Bay destinations for years without enforcement. Their defense:

### 1. Transaction happens outside the zone
"Check in: Meet us at our shop; 130 Kailua Road" — the commercial transaction (check-in, payment, equipment handoff, waivers) occurs at the shop, outside the 1,000-ft Kaneohe Bay restricted zone.

### 2. Customer self-transports
"Our friendly team will help teach you how to securely strap the kayak to your vehicle" — the customer drives themselves. No company vehicle, no staff, no equipment delivery enters the restricted zone.

### 3. Informational framing
"Location: Eastern side of Oahu, north of Kaneohe in Kualoa Regional Park (about 35 minute drive from our shop)" — this is framed as information for private boaters, not as a commercial service in the bay.

### 4. No defensive disclaimers
KBA has zero compliance banners, no legal warnings, no "IMPORTANT NOTICE" boxes. Defensive language signals guilt to both users and regulators.

## The "Decouple and Reframe" Framework

### What NOT to do
- ❌ Compliance banners ("⚠️ IMPORTANT NOTICE: Hawaii State Law prohibits...")
- ❌ TouristTrip schema on restricted destination pages (implies selling a trip there)
- ❌ "Book Now" CTAs that bundle restricted destinations into a commercial offer
- ❌ Scrubbing all mentions of the destination (loses SEO value)

### What TO do
- ✅ Keep destination content — it's valuable for SEO and users
- ✅ Embed self-transport language: "Pick up at our Kailua shop → drive yourself → launch"
- ✅ Frame as informational guide for private boaters, not commercial service
- ✅ Link to generic rental pages (never bundle destination into the product)
- ✅ TouristTrip schema is OK — the trip starts at the Kailua shop, not the beach

## Self-Transport Block (template)
```html
<div style="background:#e8f4f8;border-left:4px solid #006699;padding:20px;">
  <h3>How It Works — Pick Up & Go</h3>
  <p><strong>1. Check in at our Kailua shop</strong> (134B Hamakua Dr). We'll help load your vehicle.</p>
  <p><strong>2. Drive yourself to the launch point.</strong> You're a private boater now.</p>
  <p><strong>3. Return the gear by closing time.</strong> No delivery, no guides — just you.</p>
</div>
```

## Operational Defense (beyond website)
- GPS tracking (Bouncy) — prove company vehicles never enter Kaneohe Bay zone
- Security cameras — timestamped proof the rental happened at the shop, not the beach
- Rental agreement: "Renter is responsible for legal transport and launch"
- Staff script: "We rent gear from Kailua. Where you go is your choice as a private boater."
- Never verbally direct to restricted launch points — provide a map of ALL legal access points

## Enforcement Precedent
BLNR issued a $35,000 fine against a kayak rental company (April 2024, Spectrum News). The violation was operating/delivering in the bay, not just advertising. Advertising + documented separation from the beach = defensible.
