## Question Design Principles (from this batch)

1. **Start with sensory description.** "Describe paddling out for the first time" — grounds the answer in experience, not abstraction.
2. **Layer from practical → philosophical.** Early questions are concrete (what gear, when to go), later questions are reflective (what never gets old, why do you keep doing this).
3. **Ask for the unwritten.** "What's something no guidebook mentions?" "What drives locals crazy?" — this is the content Google can't get from structured data.
4. **Include one rescue/close-call question.** Builds credibility. Shows you're honest about risk.
5. **End on the feeling.** "What does someone feel after..." or "Describe that feeling" — emotional hooks for social content.
6. **Every script maps to a specific domain + page type.** No generic content. GRO-138 feeds yourhawaiiguide kayak guides, GRO-144 feeds activeoahutours safety page, etc.
